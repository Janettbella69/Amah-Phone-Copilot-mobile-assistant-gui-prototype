# Amah Phone Copilot-Mobile-assistant-gui-prototype
Amah Phone Copilot is an on-device mobile GUI assistant designed for elderly Chinese immigrants who struggle with English, smartphone apps, and digital services.

Many overseas Chinese seniors speak Cantonese, Teochew, Hokkien, Fuzhounese, Taishanese, or dialect-influenced English. They may not be comfortable with standard Mandarin, written English, or complex app interfaces. Everyday tasks such as booking a doctor appointment, checking a bill, shopping online, adjusting phone settings, or identifying scam messages can become stressful and risky.

Our project helps them use smartphones in the language they actually speak.

The assistant listens to the senior’s voice, understands dialect-mixed speech, reads the current phone screen, explains what the app is asking, and guides the user step by step with large buttons, simple language, and voice feedback. For low-risk tasks, it can help tap, scroll, or input information. For high-risk actions such as payment, login, sending messages, deleting content, or sharing personal information, it always asks for user or family confirmation first.

Unlike cloud-based assistants, Amah Phone Copilot runs on-device by default. Voice, screen content, medical information, banking pages, and personal messages are processed locally to protect privacy and build trust.

The goal is not to make seniors adapt to complicated apps.
The goal is to make the phone adapt to them.
